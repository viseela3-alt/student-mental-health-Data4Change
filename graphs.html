Screen Time vs. GPA

This scatter plot shows the relationship between daily phone screen time (in hours) and student GPA for all 500 participants. Each dot is one student. The downward trend indicates a negative correlation — as screen time increases, GPA tends to decrease. The line of best fit has a slope of −0.08 GPA points per hour of screen time.

Average GPA by Screen Time Group

Students were grouped into four categories based on daily screen time: under 2 hours, 2–4 hours, 4–6 hours, and over 6 hours. Students in the lowest screen time group had the highest average GPA (3.6), while those in the highest group averaged 3.1. The difference of 0.5 GPA points is statistically significant (p < 0.05).

GPA Distribution by School

Box plots comparing GPA distribution across the three participating schools. All three schools show a similar pattern, suggesting the correlation between screen time and GPA is consistent across different school environments, not specific to one school's grading policy.
